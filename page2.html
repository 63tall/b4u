A voyage back to you · Chapter 2 of 5

I made you feel like an afterthought.

I mentioned a trip to see a friend the way I'd mention any plan — without thinking about how it would land on you. Then, when you reacted, I teased you instead of listening. That wasn't playful. It turned something you actually felt into a punchline, and that's on me, not on you for being upset.

I never want us to be against each other. It's us vs. the problem, always. I value you, and I'd never knowingly do anything to risk what we have.

I should have led with that instead of a joke.
